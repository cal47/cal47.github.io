End of Week 3 and I Know That I Don't Know a Lot

Oct 4th, 2013

I know that I’m making progress and I’m enjoying the process but I’m starting to get stressed about finding the right job opportunity. So this morning, on my commute in, I watched a Ted talk about stress that made me feel better. If you watch this, watch it to the very end. The presenter is asked a question from a moderator (why Ted Talks need moderators…) and she has an excellent response.

I haven’t been a student in a long, long time and I’m finding it to be both fun and difficult! I am not used to concentrating on learning for so long. I’m very good at being a top level thinker and seeing how different parts are put together, interact with each other, interact with users and how everything flows together. However I’m not [YET] very good at programming and it’s somewhat frustrating. I know the steps I would take to get from point A to point B, but I’m so unfamiliar with how the technology works and still too inept at stringing together syntax to quickly or easily make my programs do what I want them to do. I feel that in terms of my proficiency I’m about in the middle of the pack but I have no way to gauge my progress relative to any “norm.” I would like to know what I should be able to do at certain points as we progress through the program. Like after the stackworld exercise (which was awesome btw, as it grounded how web applications are created and maintained through a tangible project), what should I be able to accomplish on my own at the end of this project? I guess I feel that I’m not progressing as fast as I should be. More accurately, I’m not progressing as fast as I want to.
