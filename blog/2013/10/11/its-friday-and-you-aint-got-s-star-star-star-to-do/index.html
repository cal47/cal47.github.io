It's Friday and You Ain't Got S*** to Do

Oct 11th, 2013

That is (obvi) a quote from the movie “Friday,” when Chris Tucker comes by Ice Cube’s house after Cube has been fired. Now for me it’s also Friday but I have a lot of s*** to do. Let’s start by revisiting the past and go over my last week @ Fullstack. It flat out sucked. I had zero energy, I could not concentrate on anything I could not focus and I was falling asleep during the lectures. By the end of the week I was frustrated at myself and questioning why I had even bothered to sign up for this. So last Friday rolls around and I decide that perhaps a lot of sleep would provide me with a reset and get me back on track. I woke up Saturday morning and still felt like complete dog dookie. I moped around for most of the day and at around 5 PM I visited my gf’s business to help her with some computer stuff. Now my girlfriend owns a massage therapy business and when i complained of a headach she offered to give me a neck rub. This neck rub literally changed my life. It turns out all my lack of focus and loss of energy was because I was carrying all my tension in my neck and this had severe nuerological consequences such as my incredible lack of focus and everything else I listed above. Frankly I’m still shocked at the difference a massage made. I feel like I lost a valuable week last week.

So when I started this week, I immediately felt 100% better than I did all of last week. I was alert, I could focus, I cared a lot more… everything was improved. As the week progressed I made up for some of the lost ground. I am amazed at the difference in progress I’ve made this week compared to last. Moving forward I expect to maintain this momentum and accelerate my learning.

At a high level, I understand the MVC patterns of Rails and I can see how they come together. It was a considerable context shift from straight Ruby but the lessons this week made the transition much easier. I actually really liked the progression we made through rails this week and rebuilding stackworld on rails was a great idea for educational purposes. It really helped crystalize how rails works. I only wish there was a more visual way of seeing how all the parts fit together.

I found that I was doing the Stackworld in rails mostly solo and I liked it. I actually restarted the project at some point b/c of environment issues and it really helped me to understand it b/c I could see the MVC patterns more clearly than I would have been able to working with a partner. I like working on team but not until I have a good foundational understanding of the concepts that I’m working with.

I approached this assessment from the wrong perspective. I did not study a lot and now I’m regretting that decision. Sigh…
